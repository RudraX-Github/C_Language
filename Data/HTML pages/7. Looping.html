1. Types of Loops & Anatomy

Loops execute a block of code repeatedly as long as a condition is true. C has three primary loop types: For, While, and Do-While. They are categorized into Entry-Controlled (Condition checked before entry) and Exit-Controlled (Condition checked after execution).

2. While vs. Do-While

The primary difference is execution guarantee. A While loop might never execute if the condition is false initially. A Do-While loop executes its body at least once before ever checking the condition.

3. Control Statements

C provides jump statements to alter the normal flow of a loop:

  • break;: Instantly terminates the entire loop and exits.
  • continue;: Skips the rest of the current iteration and jumps straight to the next iteration.
  • goto label;: Jumps to a specific marked label anywhere in the function (generally avoided in modern C, but exists).
